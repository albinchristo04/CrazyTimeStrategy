Our live Crazy Time tracker fetches the last 100 real game results from a verified data source and runs frequency analysis, gap detection, and a 4-signal prediction score on each segment.

## How to Track Today's Results

Visit the [live Crazy Time tracker](/crazy-time-predictor) and click "Refresh Results". The tool fetches the most recent 100 results and shows:

- **Frequency analysis** — actual hit rate vs expected rate per segment
- **Gap table** — how many rounds since each segment last hit vs its average gap
- **Prediction grid** — a score from 0–100 per segment based on four signals

You can also enter results manually if you are watching a live table.

## What the Data Actually Tells You

The tracker shows patterns in historical data. It does not predict individual spins — each spin is independent by design. What it does tell you:

- Which segments are running below their expected frequency (cold)
- Which segments are overdue based on their average gap
- Which segments have been hot recently

Use this as context for your [Crazy Time strategy guide](/crazy-time-strategy) decisions, not as guaranteed predictions.

## The 4 Signals Behind the Score

Each segment's prediction score combines:

1. **Base rate** — theoretical probability from the wheel design
2. **Gap boost** — overdue segments score higher
3. **Cold correction** — below-expected segments get a boost
4. **Recent dampening** — recently hot segments get a slight reduction

This is a heuristic model for educational use. It does not override the fundamental randomness of each spin.

## FAQ

**Where does the tracker data come from?**
The tracker fetches data through a server-side proxy from a verified Crazy Time results API. The source token is never exposed to the browser.

**How often is the data updated?**
On user-triggered refresh only. No auto-polling. Click "Refresh Results" to load the latest data.

**Can I enter results manually?**
Yes. The manual entry panel lets you add individual results by tapping segment buttons or typing comma-separated values.

**Does the tracker guarantee results?**
No. It is an educational pattern analysis tool. Each spin is statistically independent.

**How does the [RTP breakdown](/crazy-time-rtp) relate to tracker data?**
RTP describes long-run mathematical expectations. The tracker shows short-run frequency data. Both are useful context — neither predicts the next spin.
