Coin Flip is the most frequently triggered bonus round in Crazy Time alongside Cash Hunt and Pachinko. It appears twice on the 54-segment wheel (~3.7% per spin). This guide covers how to size your Coin Flip stake and when to target it.

## How Coin Flip Works

The wheel lands on Coin Flip. A physical coin with a red side and blue side is flipped. Each side has a random multiplier assigned. Whichever side wins, that multiplier pays your Coin Flip stake. With a top slot double active, all multipliers are doubled before the flip.

## Coin Flip Bet Sizing

A sensible Coin Flip stake is 20–30% of your total round bet. This keeps a bonus exposure active every round without over-allocating to a 3.7% probability event.

**Example — $4 total round bet:**
- On the 1 segment: $2.80
- On Coin Flip: $1.20

Use the [bankroll management guide](/crazy-time-bankroll-strategy) to calculate your total round budget first.

## When to Increase Your Coin Flip Stake

If the top slot shows "Coin Flip — double" before the spin, the bonus multipliers are doubled if triggered. Some players increase their Coin Flip stake for that specific round. This is a valid tactical adjustment within your session budget.

## Coin Flip vs Other Bonuses

Coin Flip hits at the same rate as Cash Hunt and Pachinko but has a lower max payout. For pure upside hunting, Pachinko is better. For consistent bonus exposure, Coin Flip is the easiest to allocate to. Read the [bonus rounds guide](/crazy-time-bonus-rounds) for a full comparison.

## FAQ

**How often does Coin Flip hit?**
Approximately every 27 rounds on average (3.7% per spin).

**Can I predict which side wins?**
No. The coin flip result is random — both sides are equally likely.

**What is the max Coin Flip payout?**
Over ×1000 with multipliers and top slot doubles stacked.

**Should I bet Coin Flip every round?**
Yes, with a small stake. Consistent bonus exposure every round means you capture it when it hits.

**Is Coin Flip the best bonus to target?**
For hit rate, yes. For maximum payout potential, Pachinko or the Crazy Time bonus are better. The [Crazy Time strategy guide](/crazy-time-strategy) covers this in full.
