Bankroll strategy is the most important skill in Crazy Time. The game itself is random — your edge comes entirely from how well you manage your money across sessions.

## Set a Session Bankroll

Before opening the game, decide your session bankroll — the maximum you will risk. This amount stays fixed. You do not add to it mid-session if you run low.

A good session bankroll is 50–100 times your intended bet size. If you plan to bet $2 per round, bring $100–$200.

## Bet Sizing Formula

Use a fixed percentage of your session bankroll:

- **Conservative:** 1% per round — lowest risk, longest sessions
- **Balanced:** 2% per round — recommended starting point
- **Aggressive:** 5% per round — high variance, short sessions

At 2% on a $200 bankroll: $4 per round. You have 50 rounds before busting at full stop-loss exposure. Read the full [bankroll management guide](/crazy-time-bankroll-strategy) for the detailed breakdown.

## Stop-Loss Rules

Set your stop-loss before the session starts:

- Conservative: stop at 30% loss ($60 on a $200 bankroll)
- Balanced: stop at 40% loss ($80)
- Aggressive: stop at 50% loss ($100)

When you hit the stop-loss, close the game. No exceptions.

## Take-Profit Rules

Set a take-profit target. If you hit a bonus that doubles your bankroll, consider leaving. Most players who continue after a big win return most of it. A +100% session profit is a good result — protect it.

## Common Bankroll Mistakes

- Adding money mid-session after hitting the stop-loss
- Increasing bet size after losses to recover faster
- Playing without a plan and making emotional decisions

## FAQ

**How much money do I need to start?**
Minimum 50 times your bet size. At $1 bets, bring $50. At $5 bets, bring $250.

**Can I split my bankroll across multiple days?**
Yes — dividing your total budget into daily session amounts is excellent bankroll discipline.

**What happens if I bust my session bankroll?**
Stop. Do not deposit more. Wait for your next planned session.

**Should I track my results?**
Yes. Tracking wins and losses across sessions shows your real performance and helps you adjust strategy.

**Is the [Crazy Time strategy guide](/crazy-time-strategy) or the bankroll guide more important?**
The bankroll guide. A bad strategy with good bankroll discipline beats a good strategy with no discipline.
