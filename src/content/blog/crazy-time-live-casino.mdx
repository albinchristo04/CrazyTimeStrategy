## How Crazy Time Live Casino Works — Round by Round

Understanding the structure of a live Crazy Time round helps you know what to expect before you deposit.

**Betting phase (15–20 seconds)**
A countdown timer opens the betting window. You select one or more of eight possible bets: number segments (1, 2, 5, 10) or bonus rounds (Coin Flip, Cash Hunt, Pachinko, Crazy Time). You can bet on as many segments as you like, but covering all eight simultaneously creates a guaranteed mathematical loss per round.

**Top slot spin**
Before the main wheel spins, a digital slot display above the wheel activates and randomly selects one of the eight bet types plus a multiplier (typically ×2 to ×10). If the main wheel lands on that same segment, your payout is multiplied by that value. This fires every round and creates a layer of unpredictable upside on ordinary number bets.

**Main wheel spin**
The host spins the 54-segment physical wheel. A flapper at the top of the wheel determines the winning segment as the wheel decelerates. The result is announced live.

**Payout or bonus trigger**
- If a number segment wins, bets on that number pay out at the listed multiplier (1:1, 2:1, 5:1, or 10:1). Any top slot match is applied.
- If a bonus segment wins, the game transitions to the corresponding bonus round. Only players who bet on that bonus segment participate.

**Bonus round (if triggered)**
The four bonus rounds — Coin Flip, Cash Hunt, Pachinko, and the Crazy Time bonus wheel — each play out as a mini game-show within the main show. Payouts range from a few times your bonus stake to, in rare cases, thousands of times. A full breakdown is on the [bonus rounds page](/crazy-time-bonus-rounds).

**New round begins**
The entire cycle repeats, typically every 30–45 seconds. A 60-minute session involves roughly 80–120 rounds.
